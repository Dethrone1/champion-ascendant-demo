Why These Factors

WHAT DRIVES THE SCORE

Every component in ASCENT is there because it showed up in actual signing data. Not because it sounds smart — because it predicted who got signed. The specific weights are proprietary; the factors themselves are summarized below.

ONE CHAMPIONSHIP ASCENT

dominant factor

Finish Rate

The dominant predictor. ONE Championship signs finishers — fighters who end fights by KO/TKO or submission. Finish rate alone explains more of the variance in who gets signed than any other single variable.

secondary

Win Rate

Winning matters, but less than how you win. A decision-heavy undefeated record gets credit, but not as much as a finish-heavy one. This separates "good record" from "exciting record" — and ONE skews toward exciting.

pipeline

Promotion Pipeline

Not all promotions feed equally into ONE. Pipeline strength captures both where fighters compete and where scouts pay attention, weighted by historical signing share.

regional

Origin Region Fit

Based on actual signing share by fighter's region of origin. Certain regions account for a disproportionate share of ONE's MMA signings. This is data from real signings, not subjective "appeal."

modifier

Age (Development Runway)

Younger fighters have more upside, more time to develop, and more potential fights on a contract. Older fighters are penalized — not because they can't compete, but because promotions invest in longer timelines.

bonus

Title Fight Wins

Winning championships proves you perform under pressure. Title-fight wins layer a bonus on top, rewarding proven competitive ability at the highest level of a fighter's current promotion.

DWCS ASCENT

dominant factor

Finish Rate (Normalized)

Finishing ability is the main driver for DWCS invites too. Unlike ONE, this version normalizes by weight class — because DWCS spans a wider weight range and finish rates vary significantly across divisions. The normalization levels that playing field.

secondary

Win Rate

Same logic: winning matters but doesn't dominate. Records below a threshold trigger a penalty, because DWCS rarely invites fighters with shaky records regardless of finishes.

momentum

Win Streak

DWCS rewards momentum. The streak bonus uses a diminishing-returns curve — the first few consecutive wins matter most. This reflects how matchmakers think about "hot" fighters.

pipeline

Promotion Pipeline

Feeder promotions are tiered by how many DWCS invitees came from each. Top feeders carry significant weight; regional unknowns carry little — not because they can't get invited, but the odds are much lower.

modifier

Age

DWCS has a distinct sweet spot — prospects right in their athletic prime who can develop over a UFC career. Older fighters face a steeper penalty than in the ONE pathway.

elite bonus

Elite Stack Bonuses

What separates strong fighters from blue-chip ones. Bonuses stack for elite traits like being undefeated, an elite normalized finish rate, or a long active win streak. You need to be elite across multiple traits to break into the top tier.
